CoronaBlog

Aniket's Brain
2020-04-18

I hope whoever is reading this, if there is anyone, is safe right now. We’re in some damn crazy times (thanks to COVID19) and there doesnt seem to be a lot of hope in the world right now, especially not in the USA. What really is interesting is that people still use the current state of affairs to berate others on political and personal opinions. I find it hard to believe that this is what the social media creators wanted from their platforms. Of course there are many benefits that technology provides us like having the ability to communicate with people, but its hard to imagine that people would be so vehemently ready to voice their opinions like the do online to people’s faces if they got the opportunity to discuss the qualms that are affecting both sides. I watched a video that at first had me absolutely shocked regarding citizens of Michigan protesting at the state capital due to the stay at home order. While major news networks only showed very small nitpicked examples from their interviews to show some obvious political bias, it seemed to be a completely different story when watching the interviews from the people at the local news. The protestors seemed relatively respectful and still understood that the virus was a real threat, but explained that the prospect of the shutdown going further than another few weeks would put them in economic situations that most middle to upper middle class folks just cant comprehend. I understand medically, that the virus poses major problems, but berating people for having disagreements with you on anonymous platforms is not going to provide much to solving the problem. Maybe if the problem affects you, voice your opinion, but worrying about another person’s thoughts on the other side of the country is unlikely to do you any good if you don’t have insight into what theyre thinking.

When did we change our outlook on putting people to work and having the citizens be the backbone of the country. We see that there are vast differences in the manufacturing facility standards of the countries that the US moved its production to for cheaper prices and labor, but did the government understand the fallout of removing jobs from those who lived off of those means? I would doubt it as there was no reason to fret with markets jumping higher than ever before. One thing about Trump, however ill-trained or downright stupid he may be, is that he did strike a note with the american people regarding making America great. What happened to giving people the satisfaction of living an American dream. Did we lose that when we sacrificed those with low intellectual work to outsourcing? As a coast dweller myself, I have no idea what it means to be a factory man, or a miner, or any of the such jobs that ran the American economy in the last century, but I would be curious as to whether they think that the government has their best interests at heart. We complain about giving everyone free college, and scoff at the idea of jobs that would bring normal people the satisfaction of supporting a family even if the job was unskilled labor.

I think that this whole situation will put a lot of folks’ skills into question. What would have happened if we had a robust manufacturing supply chain and pipeline for building our own supplies rather than being strangled by the whims of a microscopic organism? In the last post, I mentioned that I thought there were too many companies that were not serving a purpose other than to make lives slightly easier with small incremental changes being business purposes. Those companies are all going to die as they will quickly learn that although a smart coffee maker at 3x the price of a french press seems nice in theory, when its that or groceries for the next week, their revenue streams are going to dry up fast. Although, in opposition, it is likely to become a time to catch some sort of resurgence in America with respect to production and jobs that will help people support themselves and their families in the middle of the country. We have a massive population of people that feel as though the country has let them down simply because they have been robbed of the simple chance to support their families with a worthwile job. I was taking a look at other ways to provide people with the support they need to live comfortably and found this great look at providing people with ability to earn a living wage. These new ideas are things that have to be proposed with contrast to the few options that are generally discussed in the main stream. Hopefully we can come out of this with a new understanding of what the government can do to provide its citizens with the best chance to have a meaningful career.
